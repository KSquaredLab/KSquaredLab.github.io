Research

Research Themes

We work at the intersection of statistical methodology, biomedical data analysis, and disease-focused discovery.

Multi-omics and Epigenetics

We analyze DNA methylation, transcriptomic, genetic, and other omics data to study biological mechanisms of disease. Our work emphasizes interpretable statistical analysis and reproducible computational workflows.

COPD and Respiratory Disease Data Science

We develop data-driven approaches for respiratory disease research, including COPD. Projects may integrate clinical variables, omics profiles, imaging-derived features, and longitudinal outcomes.

Statistical Genetics and Rare Variant Analysis

We study genetic contributions to complex biomedical traits using statistical genetics methods. A particular interest is rare variant analysis and methods that connect genetic signals to disease biology.

Biomedical AI and Prediction Modeling

We build prediction models for biomedical and clinical research questions with careful attention to validation, interpretability, and practical usefulness. Our goal is to make AI methods rigorous and scientifically meaningful.

Environmental Health Data Science

We investigate how environmental exposures relate to health outcomes using statistical modeling and modern data science. This includes exposure-response modeling, biomarker discovery, and integration with biomedical datasets.

We are recruiting motivated Master’s students

K² Lab welcomes students who want to learn how statistics, data science, bioinformatics, and biomedical AI can be used to understand complex biomedical data. Prior experience is helpful, but curiosity, consistency, and willingness to learn matter most.

Research interests

  • Statistics
  • Data science
  • Biomedical data analysis
  • Bioinformatics
  • AI/ML for healthcare
  • Omics and environmental health

Students will learn

  • R and Python
  • Regression and survival analysis
  • GWAS and rare variant analysis
  • DNA methylation and multi-omics analysis
  • Machine learning and AI models
  • Scientific writing and reproducible research
