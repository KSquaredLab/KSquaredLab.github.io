Kangjin Kim Lab for Biostatistics, Bioinformatics, and Biomedical AI

K² Lab

Turning biomedical data into knowledge

K² represents Kangjin Kim and knowledge discovery from biomedical data. Our lab develops statistically rigorous and computationally reproducible methods for high-dimensional biomedical data, with a focus on multi-omics, statistical genetics, respiratory disease, environmental health, and biomedical AI.

Led by Kangjin Kim, DPH, whose postdoctoral research training includes Harvard Medical School and Brigham and Women’s Hospital, the lab emphasizes biostatistics, statistical modeling, survival and prediction analysis, rare variant analysis, and reproducible R/Python workflows.

We are recruiting motivated Master’s students interested in statistics, data science, bioinformatics, biomedical AI, and quantitative biomedical research.

Research

Biostatistics and Biomedical Data Science

We work from a biostatistical foundation: careful study design, modeling, inference, prediction, validation, and reproducible computation for complex biomedical datasets.

Statistical Core Expertise

  • Statistical modeling and inference Regression, mixed models, longitudinal data analysis, uncertainty quantification, and interpretable inference.
  • Survival analysis and prediction modeling Risk prediction, model validation, calibration, time-to-event outcomes, and clinical interpretability.
  • Statistical genetics and rare variant analysis Whole-genome sequencing, gene-based testing, variant annotation, PRS-adjusted association analysis, and multi-ancestry data.
  • Reproducible R/Python biomedical analysis Transparent workflows, simulation studies, software development, and documented analyses for collaborative research.

Multi-omics and Epigenetics

We analyze DNA methylation, transcriptomic, genetic, and other omics data to study biological mechanisms of disease. Our work emphasizes high-dimensional data integration, covariate-aware modeling, interpretable statistical analysis, and reproducible computational workflows.

COPD and Respiratory Disease Data Science

Building on postdoctoral research at Harvard Medical School and Brigham and Women’s Hospital, we develop data-driven approaches for respiratory disease research, including COPD. Projects may integrate clinical variables, whole-genome sequencing, proteomics, imaging-derived features, and longitudinal outcomes.

Statistical Genetics and Rare Variant Analysis

We study genetic contributions to complex biomedical traits using statistical genetics methods. A particular interest is rare variant analysis, gene-based tests, multi-ancestry whole-genome sequencing analysis, and methods that connect genetic signals to disease biology.

Biomedical AI and Prediction Modeling

We build prediction models for biomedical and clinical research questions with careful attention to statistical validation, calibration, interpretability, and practical usefulness. Our goal is to make AI methods rigorous and scientifically meaningful.

Environmental Health Data Science

We investigate how environmental exposures relate to health outcomes using statistical modeling and modern data science. This includes exposure-response modeling, biomarker discovery, and integration with biomedical datasets.
